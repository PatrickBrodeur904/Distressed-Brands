The Brand Life Cycle & Where These Brands Live
All brands in this analysis occupy the distressed zone — former category leaders now available for revitalization.
Universe of Brands
Scope of Analysis
This analysis surveys U.S. health, wellness, beauty, and personal care brands that once commanded meaningful category market share — defined as at least 5% of their respective category — but have since fallen below 1% market share in 2026. The universe spans brands whose peak popularity occurred after 1970, with an emphasis on brands that peaked between the 1990s and 2010s, as these represent the deepest wells of consumer nostalgia and residual name recognition. The dataset also includes an exception category for DTC lifestyle health brands such as Jenny Craig.
Selection Criteria
How We Identified Candidates
  • ✓Peak U.S. category market share >5% at any point after 1970
  • ✓Current U.S. category market share <1% as of 2026
  • ✓Sales or market share declined ≥80% from brand peak
  • ✓Peak popularity occurred post-1970, indexed toward 1990s–2010s
  • ✓High aided brand awareness among U.S. consumers (>40%)
  • ✓Exception: DTC lifestyle health brands (e.g., Jenny Craig) included regardless of OTC market share metrics
Exclusion Filters
What We Ruled Out
  • ✕Brands with estimated current valuation >$250M (e.g., Revlon)
  • ✕Brands whose decline was primarily driven by FDA regulation or OTC compliance failure
  • ✕Brands still operating at or near peak levels
  • ✕Brands with severe unresolved reputational damage (safety recalls, active litigation)
  • ✕Private label, store-brand, or white-label derivatives without standalone brand equity
  • ✕Micro-brands lacking sufficient consumer awareness data
Methodology Note
How to Read This Report
Brands are organized into three tiers on the Brand Analysis tab: Recommended for Consideration (top candidates), Brand Overview (viable, require more diligence), and the Brand List (complete evaluated universe). Brands promoted to the top two tiers appear in a structured comparison table. All tier assignments are saved automatically. Use filters, search, and the compare tool to explore the full universe.
All data reflects publicly available information as of Q1 2026. Brand financial metrics are estimated based on available filings, press reports, and industry databases. This analysis is intended for internal strategic discussion only.
